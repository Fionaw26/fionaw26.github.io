My New York Essay

New York City is often described as fast, relentless, and impossible to slow down. Trains rattle beneath the pavement, traffic lights flicker between red and green, and footsteps echo endlessly against the sidewalk. To live here is to move.

But beneath that constant motion, there are quieter moments: the three seconds when the red light pauses traffic, the elders gathered in ColumbusPark, the unexpected stillness during a lunch break. New York city is not just a city of speed, but also a place where meaning reveals itself in the pauses between the rush.
